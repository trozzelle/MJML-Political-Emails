A Note From Nate

The past few months we’ve seen lots of unrest - millions of Americans are calling for justice, and notably so this past week in Rochester where many residents of NY-27 work, have family, attend school, and spend much of their time. This time we are crying out for justice right here in our own community. More than five months after Rochester Police officers murdered a 41-year-old Black man, Daniel Prude, we have just learned much more about the circumstances of what led to his death. We are outraged, we want answers, and we want change.
Recently, I was honored to receive the endorsement of Persist Brooklyn. The support from NYC is meaningful because they recognize the importance of our whole state having representation that cares about working people, not just the typically blue areas.

Today is Labor Day. I hope you’re able to rest today and reflect on what the labor movement has won for us. Things we take for granted like the weekend, overtime, paid leave, child labor laws were fought for with protests, strikes, and uprisings. It gives me confidence that our movements today use tactics and inspiration from those important fights. It helps me know that we will win.

In my candidacy for NY-27, I’ve earned the endorsement and the trust of many local labor organizations. My father was in the Painters Union before he passed away at 39. Even then, it was clear what a difference having a union job made for my parents and six siblings. I promise to center my efforts in Congress on workers, their healthcare, and their struggles when I’m elected.

COVID-19

These days, the news is mixed: New York’s Governor Cuomo advised this week that COVID-19 hospitalizations have dropped to the lowest number since March 16 when the state was the epicenter of the virus, and the state’s COVID-19 infection rate has been less than 1% for 30 straight days. But Western New York is reporting the highest infection rate in the state at 2% (Long Island reports 1.1%, and all other regions in the state are below 1%, according to the NYS’s metrics).

School districts are opening up, colleges and universities are cautiously welcoming students back onto campus under strict guidelines, while businesses continue phasing back into our lives. It is the biggest transition since the pandemic began, and remaining vigilant with masking and social distancing continues to be extremely important. I am so proud of how our community and our campaign continues to offer support where we can. Please contact us if you need. We are happy to help!
